Philosophy

The studio's work is guided by a "design + research" parallel approach, exploring the architectural language of Asia by emphasizing the analogy between nature and "man-made nature." Their primary goal is to discover a new type of natural architecture derived from the unique characteristics of the city itself, focusing on providing an improvised, adaptable, and meticulously crafted architectural language.

Studio Insights

Design Approach

HAS Design and Research led by visionary architects Jenchieh Hung and Kulthida Songkittipakdee, blends local culture, climate, and context to create sustainable, innovative spaces that enhance user experiences and respect the environment.

Important Principle

We believe architecture should always respond to its context—climate, culture, and site—creating designs that are both relevant and meaningful.

How do you approach residential or small-scale projects?

For residential projects, architects Jenchieh Hung and Kulthida Songkittipakdee focus on functionality, comfort, and personalization, tailoring each design to the client's needs while maintaining a distinct architectural identity.

What do you value most in the relationship between architecture and its context?

We value how architecture can enhance and respect its surroundings. The relationship between the built environment and its context should be harmonious and mutually enriching.
